SWAMP DEER
Swamp deer, also known as Barasingha, are medium-sized deer native to the Indian subcontinent. They thrive in wetland habitats, characterized by their long legs and distinctive antlers with multiple tines. Known for their graceful movements, they are herbivores, feeding on grasses and aquatic plants, and are considered vulnerable due to habitat loss.

ELEPHANT
Elephants are the largest land mammals, known for their intelligence, strong social bonds, and long memories. They live in herds led by matriarchs and communicate using low-frequency sounds. Their tusks, used for digging and foraging, are endangered due to poaching. Elephants play a crucial role in their ecosystems.
